Sponsor key provisioning
========================

The bridge's /sponsor endpoint co-signs transactions and pays gas on behalf of
zkLogin users. It needs an Ed25519 keypair whose Sui address is funded with
SUI for gas. The key is loaded at startup from the env var
SPONSOR_PRIVATE_KEY_B64. If the var is missing, the bridge still starts but
/sponsor returns HTTP 503 with a clear error.

1) Generate a fresh Ed25519 keypair locally:

       sui client new-address ed25519 sponsor

   This prints the new address and stores the key in your Sui keystore
   (~/.sui/sui_config/sui.keystore).

2) Export the base64-encoded 32-byte secret seed. Two options:

   a) Read it directly from the keystore. Each entry is a base64 string whose
      first byte is the key-scheme flag (0x00 for Ed25519) followed by the
      32-byte seed:

          jq -r '.[]' ~/.sui/sui_config/sui.keystore
          # then identify the line matching your sponsor address

      The bridge's loader accepts either the raw 32-byte seed in base64 or the
      33-byte flag+seed form; it strips the flag automatically.

   b) Use the keytool export (newer Sui CLIs):

          sui keytool export --key-identity <sponsor-address>

      Copy the base64 secret-key field.

3) Drop it into your .env file:

       SPONSOR_PRIVATE_KEY_B64=<base64-from-step-2>

   See .env.example for the full set of supported vars.

4) Fund the sponsor address on testnet so it has SUI for gas. The testnet
   faucet works once per address per cooldown window:

       curl -X POST https://faucet.testnet.sui.io/v2/gas \
         -H 'Content-Type: application/json' \
         -d '{"FixedAmountRequest":{"recipient":"<sponsor-address>"}}'

5) Restart the bridge. On startup it logs:

       sponsor key loaded: 0x...

   You can also hit GET /health to confirm the sponsor address is loaded.

Operational notes
-----------------
- Treat SPONSOR_PRIVATE_KEY_B64 like any other production secret. Use the
  hosting provider's secret manager rather than committing .env.
- The sponsor pays gas for every /sponsor call. Monitor its balance and top
  it up before it drains.
- The bridge does no rate limiting or fraud checks on /sponsor. Put one of
  those in front of it (per-IP limit, per-address allowlist, daily budget)
  before exposing it publicly.
